Fourier Image Compressor
Have you ever wondered how you can make your images smaller without losing too much quality? Sometimes you might want to do this to save space on your device or to share your images faster online. In this article, we will introduce you to a cool app that lets you do just that:
Fourier Image Compressor is based on a clever math trick called Fourier transform. This trick allows us to change any image into bunch of waves with different colors and strengths. These waves are called frequency components and they represent how much each color changes across an image. For example high frequency component means that color changes rapidly from one pixel to another while low frequency component means that color changes slowly or stays same. The amazing thing about Fourier transform is that we can go back and forth between original image and frequency components without losing any information. This means that we can reconstruct our image exactly as it was by adding up all waves together. However we can also choose to keep only some of waves and discard rest. This will make our image smaller but also less accurate. That's where Fourier Image Compressor comes in handy. It lets you pick how much data you want to keep and shows you how your image looks like with different levels of compression. You can also compare your compressed image with original one and see how much space you saved. To use Fourier Image Compressor simply upload your image file and move slider to adjust data level. You will see compressed image on right side and original image on left side. You can also download compressed image as PNG file by clicking on download button. Fourier Image Compressor works best with images that have smooth gradients and few sharp edges. Images that have lots of details and noise will not compress well and may look distorted or blurry. Try it out with different images and see how it works for yourself!
