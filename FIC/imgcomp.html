Image compression

How does it work?

Have you ever wondered how you can make your images smaller without losing too much quality? Sometimes you might want to do this to save space on your device or to share your images faster online. In this article, we will introduce you to a cool app that lets you do just that: Fourier Image Compressor.

Fourier Image Compressor is based on a clever math trick called the Fourier transform. This trick allows us to change any image into a bunch of waves with different colors and strengths. These waves are called frequency components and they represent how much each color changes across the image. For example, a high-frequency component means that the color changes rapidly from one pixel to another, while a low-frequency component means that the color changes slowly or stays the same.

The amazing thing about the Fourier transform is that we can go back and forth between the original image and the frequency components without losing any information. This means that we can reconstruct the image exactly as it was by adding up all the waves together. However, we can also choose to keep only some of the waves and discard the rest. This will make our image smaller but also less accurate.

That's where Fourier Image Compressor comes in handy. It lets you pick how many waves you want to keep by putting in the % of data you want to keep. The more waves you keep, the better your image looks and the bigger it is. The fewer waves you keep, the worse your image looks and the smaller it is. You can see how this affects your image by using a slider on the app's interface. You can also compare your compressed image with the original one side by side.

Fourier Image Compressor is a fun and easy way to learn about image compression and Fourier transforms. You can experiment with different images and see how they change when you vary the % of data you keep. You can also save your compressed images and share them with others if you like.
